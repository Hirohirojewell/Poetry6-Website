Whispers of Winter

Softly, gently, they fall from the sky,
Tiny whispers, a silent sigh.
Dancing down without a sound,
Blanketing softly the world around.

Each flake unique, a fleeting grace,
A frozen wonder in endless space.
They twirl and drift in winter's embrace,
Skyflakes in a gentle, quiet race.

Upon the earth, a quilt they lay,
Turning night into softer day.
A world transformed by icy art,
A winter's gift to warm the heart.

Beneath the stars, in stillness deep,
The earth in silent beauty sleeps.
The skyflakes fall, a winter song,
In nature’s choir, where they belong.

Embrace the cold, embrace the light,
Each flake a spark in velvet night.
A gentle hush, a tender glow,
As skyflakes dance, so soft, so slow.
